Why this generator

Fair by maths, private by design

Many online pickers lean on Math.random(), which isn't built to be unpredictable. Ours uses your device's cryptographically-secure randomness — the same class trusted for encryption keys — and we use rejection sampling so no number is quietly favoured over another. Your picks are made on your device and never transmitted anywhere.

Worth saying plainly: a generator can't predict a draw or improve your odds. Every Super Lotto combination always has exactly the same chance — and at 1 in 21,425,712 the jackpot is far more winnable than Powerball or EuroMillions, even though China is the world’s biggest lottery market by sales. What this gives you is a clean, unbiased, genuinely random line — and the option to take it further with our SHA-256 entropy generators, where your own mouse movement seeds the result.

State-run · unique rules

How China's lottery actually works

Mainland China bans almost all gambling. The only large-scale legal exceptions are two state-run systems: the China Welfare Lottery (中国福利彩票 / 福彩) and the China Sports Lottery (中国体育彩票 / 体彩). Super Lotto (大乐透) is the flagship draw of the China Sports Lottery (体彩). Both run official sales, draws and prize rules under tight government supervision, with proceeds earmarked for public causes — social-welfare projects for 福彩, sport and public-fitness programmes for 体彩.

A few things make China genuinely different from Western lotteries:

  • Physical tickets only. Online lottery sales have been banned since 2015, so 95%+ of tickets are bought in cash at 200,000+ licensed outlets — you pay face value, no brokerage fee.
  • Strictly 18+. Tickets aren't sold to minors and prizes aren't paid to minors.
  • Winnings are taxed. A single prize over ¥10,000 carries 20% personal income tax; smaller wins are tax-free.
  • The world's biggest market. China booked roughly $418 billion in lottery sales in 2023 — more than any other country.
💴 A quick note on the Yuan (¥). Tickets are priced in Chinese currency. Its official name is the renminbi (人民币, “the people's currency”, ISO code CNY); the yuan (元 / ¥) is the main unit — much like “sterling” is the currency and the “pound” the unit. One yuan divides into 10 jiao or 100 fen. As of 2026 it's roughly ¥7 to the US dollar and ¥9 to the pound, so a ¥2 ticket is about $0.29 / £0.22 — pocket change. Exchange rates move daily.
FAQ

Questions, answered

How does the Super Lotto generator work?
It picks 5 unique main numbers from 1–35 and 2 back numbers from 1–12 using your device's cryptographically-secure random generator (crypto.getRandomValues) with rejection sampling, so every number is equally likely. Generate multiple lines and copy each with one tap.
What is Super Lotto and how much is a ticket?
The China Sports Lottery flagship (超级大乐透 / Da Le Tou): 5 front-zone numbers from 1–35 + 2 back-zone numbers from 1–12, ¥2 per bet. Draws Mon, Wed & Sat at ~20:30 Beijing time. Jackpot odds are 1 in 21,425,712; an optional additional bet (+¥1) boosts prizes. Record jackpot ~¥497M.
Can a generator improve my chances of winning?
No. Super Lotto is a game of pure chance and every combination always has exactly the same probability. A generator simply gives you a fair, unbiased random line.
Are my generated numbers private?
Yes. The generator runs entirely in your browser. Your numbers are never sent to a server and no sign-up is required.

Play for fun, play responsibly

Super Lotto (大乐透) is a game of pure chance, run by the China Sports Lottery. This generator produces random combinations only — it does not predict draws or improve your odds, and every combination is equally likely. In mainland China, tickets are sold only at licensed physical outlets (online lottery sales have been banned since 2015), and only the official operator confirms a result or prize.

Play responsibly and only spend what you can afford to lose. Must be 18+ (or your local legal age). Support: GamCare, BeGambleAware, or your local helpline.
