⏳ The Preparation Machine

A penny a day adds up. Set an amount and how often you'd invest it — drip-fed from your start point to today — and watch discipline compound. The motto, made math.

💡 Try this: set it to the amount you'd actually spend, and a start date you remember. The "what if" hits different when the numbers are yours.

What this is. A dollar-cost-averaging (DCA) simulator. It takes a fixed amount and “buys” that much of the coin at the real historical price on every interval from your start date until today, then adds up the coins you’d hold and what they’d be worth now. DCA is the disciplined, automatic version of investing — you buy through the highs and the lows instead of trying to time the market.

Why it matters. The point isn’t the eye-watering Bitcoin numbers — it’s the principle. Small, consistent action compounds, and the longer you stay consistent the more the result is driven by time rather than timing. Past performance never predicts the future, the figures assume you actually held everything through every crash, and this is for learning, not financial advice.

What this is. A single lump sum dropped into the coin on your start date, tracked two ways: what it would be worth today, and the worst drawdown along the journey — the deepest peak-to-trough fall you’d have had to sit through without selling.

Why it matters. Hindsight makes every chart look easy; the drawdown is the honest part. A 40× return means nothing if you’d have panic-sold during an 80% crash. “Fail to prepare, prepare to fail” applies to your mindset as much as your money. Crypto is highly volatile and can lose most or all of its value — this is illustrative, not advice.

06

⚔️ Beat the House

Everyone gets the same provably-fair "lucky line" each week, derived from a published seed. Generate your own line, reveal the week's line, and see how many you match. Post your score — can you beat the host?

🎯 Your move: everyone gets the same provably-fair line this week. Draw yours, reveal it, see if you can beat the house — then share your score.

What this is. Each week a single “house line” is generated from a published seed using SHA-256, so it’s identical for everyone and anyone can verify after the fact that it wasn’t rigged. You draw your own line using your device’s secure randomness, then reveal and count your matches.

Why it matters. It’s a free, no-stakes game — no money, no prizes, no purchase, just bragging rights. “Provably fair” means the math is transparent, not that anyone has an edge: every line, including the house’s and yours, has exactly the same odds. That transparency is the whole point.
